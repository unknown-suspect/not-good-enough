Frequently Asked Questions / DIY Tips

What are the benefits of asphalt pavement?

• Asphalt is 20-40% cheaper than concrete. • It is safe, economical, and durable. • Asphalt pavement is fully recyclable. • It provides a smooth, quiet, and safe ride. • Properly maintained asphalt enhances vehicle safety with a skid-resistant surface.

What is the Difference Between Asphalt and Concrete?

Asphalt and concrete are essential construction materials, each with unique properties. Asphalt is a mixture of aggregate and bitumen, a petroleum-based substance, making it flexible and ideal for roads and driveways. Concrete, composed of aggregate, cement, and water, hardens into a durable, rock-like material, suitable for structures like sidewalks, patios, and foundations.

Asphalt is commonly used for tasks such as roofing, driveways, footpaths, and roads. It softens and becomes pliable under high temperatures. Concrete, however, is applied cold and is a harder material that does not soften with heat. It is typically used for structures like foundations, sidewalks, and patios.

An easy way to differentiate the two is by appearance: asphalt is usually black or brown with a distinctive texture, while concrete is gray and resembles rock.

How widely is asphalt used?

Approximately 94% of the nation's roads and highways are paved with asphalt, making it the most commonly used material for road construction.

Are asphalt pavements quieter than other types of pavements?

Yes, there is considerable research that shows that asphalt pavements tend to be quieter than concrete pavements overall.

What causes asphalt deterioration?

Asphalt deterioration is commonly caused by factors such as oxidation, poor drainage leading to water accumulation in low-lying areas, excessive weight loads, and exposure to vehicle oil and gasoline. Over time, water pooling can result in severe cracking or "alligatoring," a term used in the paving industry. Heavy loads may cause the asphalt to sink, while oil and gasoline seepage further accelerate its degradation.

With regular maintenance and proper care, an asphalt driveway can last between 20 to 30 years, ensuring durability and long-term value.
